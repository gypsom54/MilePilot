# MP-022 — Brand Messaging & AutoPilot Philosophy

**Status:** Active (copy guide)  
**North Star:** See **`docs/MP-023-NORTH-STAR.md`** for the master product philosophy.

**Type:** Copy refinement (not a UI redesign)

---

## Core positioning

MilePilot is **not** a mileage tracker.

MilePilot is an **AutoPilot for business mileage and business records**.

**Product promise:** Set it up once. MilePilot does the rest.

Every piece of copy must reinforce this.

---

## Tagline (permanent)

**Drive • Track • Claim**

Do not change.

---

## Secondary messages

Avoid "driving business" language. Use universal self-employed professional wording:

| Context | Preferred line |
|---------|----------------|
| Welcome headline | Every business mile. **On AutoPilot.** |
| Promise / signature | Run your business. **On AutoPilot.** |
| Plan pick | Every business mile. On AutoPilot. |
| Dashboard default | Business travel. On AutoPilot. |

Choose the line that best fits each screen while remaining universal.

Never imply the app is only for taxi or delivery drivers.

---

## Copy style — outcomes over features

| Avoid | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Track mileage | Never forget another business mile |
| Generate reports | Reports are ready when you need them |
| Export PDF | Your records are already prepared |
| Save trip | Everything is saved automatically |

---

## AutoPilot voice

Whenever automation happens, reinforce that it works **quietly in the background**.

The user should feel: *"I don't need to think about this."*

---

## Voice

Calm. Reassuring. Professional.

No technical overwhelm. Focus on confidence, automation and removing admin.

---

## Golden rule

Every screen answers:

> How does MilePilot make my life easier?

If copy talks about features instead of benefits — rewrite it.

We are selling **peace of mind**, not software.
