# MilePilot Voice Guide

This document defines how MilePilot speaks. Follow it for every screen, email, website line, and notification.

We sell two products:

1. **The software** — GPS, reports, tracking, dashboard.
2. **Confidence** — knowing mileage is recorded, reports are ready, tax records are organised, and the business is understood.

People buy Product 2. Product 1 delivers it.

## Onboarding voice journey

Each screen builds momentum. One idea per screen. Conversation, not forms.

| Screen | Voice |
|--------|-------|
| **MP-001** | Your driving business. On auto pilot. |
| **MP-002** | Great to meet you. What are you driving today? |
| **MP-003** | You're all set. Now let's make every business mile count. |
| **MP-004** | Record every business journey automatically. Your location is never shared. |
| **MP-005** | Never forget to start tracking. Remind when your working day begins. |

When someone finishes onboarding, they should feel like they **joined** something — not that they finished setup. The **Complete Setup →** button never mentions the dashboard; the Command Centre simply appears.

---

## Writing rules

1. **Calm over clever.** No shouting. No hype.
2. **Tangible over vague.** Say “Professional PDF reports” not “Professional reports” — PDF tells the user they can send files to an accountant.
3. **Outcome over feature.** “Never miss another business mile” beats “Automatic mileage tracking.”
4. **One screen, one idea.** Do not stack messages.
5. **Universal language.** Write for taxi drivers, couriers, trades, estate agents, mobile hairdressers, engineers, and delivery drivers — not one niche.
6. **Confidence, not compliance.** HMRC matters, but the feeling we sell is control and clarity.

---

## Words we avoid

- “Just” (diminishes the product)
- “Simple” (let the experience prove it)
- Feature lists on emotional screens
- Jargon: GPS tracking enabled, sync, backend, HMRC compliance module

---

## Tone check

Before locking any line, ask:

1. Does this build confidence?
2. Could a driver understand it in three seconds?
3. Would I say this to someone face to face?
4. Is this a feature — or a feeling?

If it is only a feature, rewrite until it earns its place.
